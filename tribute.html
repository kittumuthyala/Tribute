Tribute to Mother Teresa

Introduction

Mother Teresa, known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was an Albanian-Indian Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She dedicated her life to serving the poor and destitute around the world, and her selfless actions continue to inspire millions.

Early Life

Mother Teresa was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, now the capital of North Macedonia. At the age of 18, she left home to join the Sisters of Loreto, an Irish community of nuns with missions in India. She took the name Sister Mary Teresa after St. Thérèse of Lisieux.

Missionary Work

In 1948, Mother Teresa received a "call within a call" to leave the convent and help the poor while living among them. She established the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, a religious congregation dedicated to serving "the hungry, the naked, the homeless, the crippled, the blind, the lepers, all those people who feel unwanted, unloved, uncared for throughout society, people that have become a burden to the society and are shunned by everyone."

Legacy

Mother Teresa's legacy of love and compassion continues to inspire people worldwide. She received numerous awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. After her death on September 5, 1997, she was beatified as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta" by Pope John Paul II. She was canonized as a saint by Pope Francis on September 4, 2016.

"Spread love everywhere you go. Let no one ever come to you without leaving happier." - Mother Teresa
